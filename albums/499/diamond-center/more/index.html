    Reviews for Diamond Center

    by C/A/T


     Back to album

    After a one year, self-imposed hiatus, Ben Arp brings his dark electronic act, C/A/T, back with the new EP "Diamond Center".

    Heavily influenced by 'witch house' and the more experimental days of 'industrial music', "Diamond Center" reflects a new direction for Arp's always evolving project.

    "I think of this new rebirth of C/A/T as kind of a "gritty reboot" of the project," Arp said. "I'm really going back to basics and approaching producing how I used to when C/A/T was an entirely experimental project in the late 1990s."

    That experimental tone is probably most reflected in the EP's final track, 'Opaque', which is a six minute long dark ambient and drone based affair.

    "I think in a lot of ways, I'm sort of wearing my new influences on my sleeve a bit with this EP, but I don't necessarily think that is a bad thing. Assembling these songs was the most fun I've had in the studio in years."

    Look for a full length album by C/A/T to follow in late 2012 or early 2013.

    released 06 June 2012 all production by Ben Arp recorded in May 2012 thanks to the new breed of dark electronic artists that inspired this and future C/A/T releases. without you i would not still be producing this project.
