    Florida's Telethon makes the highest fidelty lo-fi music you've ever heard. The melodic products of Matt Kamm's mind are always slightly askew, but in the most otherworldly, brilliant ways imaginable. The effervescent embodiment of the perfect rock n' roll chameleon, Telethon is always ON enough to make you forget just how weirdly titillating his beach buggy electronic convergence actually is, until it REALLY kicks in, twisting the world in vast latitudinal paths, tearing the known sonic poles of pop soundscapes asunder. Already making a name for himself by opening up for a who's who of the independent set (including the legendary R. Stevie Moore, Yacht, Surfer Blood, Octopus Project and Mt. Eerie) Telethon has emerged as a full scale sound armada. Equal parts synth punk and indie pop. Telethon is the crystallized harmonic intelligence of a long lost future that hasn't quite been invented yet, a Post-Utopian, serendipitous, sea borne dream that bleeds the quintessence of brutal, loving, honesty and vitality.
