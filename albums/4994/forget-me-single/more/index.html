    Reviews for Forget Me - Single

    by Dozens


     Back to album

    2nd single from the upcoming full length release by Dozens

    Press Quotes:

    "Forget Me is like a sonic chigger - burrowing deep in our ears and refusing to let go. Not a difficult thing considering this Chicago trio knows all the right indie pop buttons to push - sweeping 80s’ synths, head bobbing beats...it’s pure indie pop bait and worth every listen." - Transient Jukebox

    "The intro of “Forget Me” could easily find itself on a MGMT record, but the track’s funky, dream pop vibe could be more accurately described as Hall & Oates with more sweeping, upbeat synths. Yes, Forget Me's beat bounces in all the right places while the vocals get serious and soulful." - Loud Loop Press

    “Forget Me” hits all the right catchy indie-pop buttons, its 80′s-hazed supply of dreamy synths, slight touch of white boy digi-funk and Chris Martin-ish peaks of falsetto making the tune a fetching slice of relationship soap opera drama one won’t mind hearing again and again." - Mixtape Maestro

    "These synthpop scientists have acquired every ingredient to a romance song and masterfully concocted “Forget Me” for all of us to belt aloud. If I had a girlfriend, I would break up with her just so I could live this song." - Friends With Both Arms

    "This is the kind of tune you belt out loud in the shower or in your car as fellow motorists speed by you because you’re doing 50 in a 65 and glance at you with a look that says “Hey Mariah Carey, get off the road before you kill someone” - MusicalHiFive
