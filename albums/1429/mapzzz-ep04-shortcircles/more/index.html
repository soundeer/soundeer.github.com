    Reviews for Mapzzz EP04 - shortcircles

    by Forest Floor


     Back to album

    The music of Matthew Tammariello (a.k.a. shortcircles) has had a bit of a fabled past with the Mapzzz crew. As one of the first artists that came to mind for our free, bi-weekly EP series when we launched it back in February, we've had the opprotunity (via a number of emails) to hear these tracks go from rough sketches and loose ideas to full blown compositions. Tammariello's productions seem to have carved themselves into a unique place between instrumental hip-hop (think Teebs, Bonobo, and the likes) and blissful techno (think Fourtet, Gold Panda, and the likes), incorporating dense soundscapes and sparkling melodies along with deep bass and starkly minimal beats. Vocals come in the form of chopped and pitched samples, mostly revolving around the theme of love in such an ambiguous way it's hard to tell whether they are intended to be inspiring or melancholy. But perhaps the point is for us to forge our own emotional path based on the music at hand, with its wide open spaces and lush production, shortcircles leads us to a place where we can sit alone and contemplate, bringing us out only at certain moments to remind us where we are and how we got here. -via

    released 15 March 2011 all music by shortcircles. Additional guitar provided by former Mapzzz EP artist Empty Pockets and additional vocals provided by former Mapzzz EP artist Forest Floor.
