    Reviews for cobus green

    by the rutabega


     Back to album

    contact: theseknottylines[at]gmail[dot]com

    this was the beginning of the rutabega as a concept/thing/band. i had been playing in Space & Noise Productions - a noisy Midwestern 5 piece rock machine - and these strange songs started coming out. they didn't seem to fit what we were doing at S&N inc. i started recording on a borrowed 4 track cassette recorder. then the songs started coming true. after a bit, i had a little dysfunctional family of songs. they made me feel sick, but i liked it. i sent them to Marc at Johann's Face and he said he would like to put out an album. mister Morgan helped me wrap it up.

    i sometimes think of this as my divorce album. listening to it now is tough. i still like to play a couple of these songs. 'imaginary friend' is one of my favorite recordings i've ever done.

    it's kind of a mess, but it's my mess.

    ps: my dad used to call me 'the rutabega' when i was a kid. not sure why.

    pps: i didn't realize i had misspelled 'the rutabega' till after we had 1,000 CDs sitting in my living room. you would have thought i would have googled it... they had Google back then, right?

    released 01 January 2002 Johann's Face Records | Patsy Presents All songs copyright 2001, 2002 - Joshua Wayne Hensley Music (ASCAP). All songs recorded to 4-track cassette by Joshua in his bathroom, bedroom, and frontroom... except songs 1, 9, & 11 - recorded by Matt Morgan and Joshua at Matt's home studio. All songs written and performed by Joshua except: drums and keys on 1 by Matt Morgan; stand-up bass on 2, 5, & 8 by Pat O'Hara; random percussion on 9 by Morgan; drums on 11 by Justin Thompson. thank you
