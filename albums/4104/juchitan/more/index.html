    Reviews for Juchitán

    by Change Leopardon


     Back to album

    "His debut EP, Juchitán, is unapologetic as it shifts from the drone-heavy distortion of “Fighty Cat” to the feedback-laced minimalism on “Cenotes.” There are some recurring themes among the 6-song EP, though: samples, reverb, extremely obscured vocals, and danceable tempos." - Anthony Fantano, The Needle Drop ( )

    "The collected EP, entitled Juchitán, is a six track, 26-minute affair that meanders a bit, towards the noisiest Xiu Xiu homeless rambling in 'Fighty Cat' and infectious blog-beat guitar tropics in 'Canvas Shoes' and out on a limb to cavernous infinite delay vocals on 'Watch the Sun Grow Old'. Highly recommended." - Jeremy Krinsley, Impose Magazine ( )

    Featured on Impose Magazine's list of best releases of June 2010 ( )

    "His Myspace about section puts it better than I can, but when I listen to his record Juchitan I feel like those seconds flying through the air after being hit by a car. In the good way. You know?" - Kyle, Camp Revival ( )

    released 01 June 2010
